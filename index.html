TOP PROBLEM – Information Overload & Confusion

Why this website is needed

People face information overload, fake news and scams every day, and many are not trained to evaluate online images, videos or expert-looking advice [web:64][web:67]. This increases the risk of fraud, stress and wrong decisions.

  • Fake news spreads fast through social media and messaging apps [web:64][web:66].
  • Images and videos are often edited or taken out of context [web:65][web:70].
  • Deepfake technology makes fake videos and voices look real [web:68][web:82].

SafeInfo is designed as that “one simple, trusted platform” that explains what is true, what is safe and what is important.

Tips & Resources

Practical Tips You Can Use Today

Online Safety Essentials

  • Use long, unique passwords and enable 2‑factor authentication [web:54][web:60].
  • Never share OTP, card CVV or UPI PIN with anyone.
  • Update your phone, browser and apps regularly [web:63][web:72].
Security basics

Verify Images & Videos

  • Use Google Reverse Image Search or similar tools to find the original source [web:65][web:70][web:71].
  • Look for strange shadows, warped backgrounds or lip‑sync issues in videos (deepfake clues) [web:68][web:82].
  • If a shocking clip appears from nowhere, check if trusted outlets mention it.
Fake media detection

Reliable Info Sources

  • Prefer official government portals and verified news sites [web:67].
  • Cross‑check big claims on at least two independent sources.
  • Use fact‑check websites for viral messages.
Trusted sources
Fake / AI Image & Video Check (External Tools)

If a shocking photo or video looks “too perfect”, you can upload it to dedicated deepfake / AI‑image detectors that analyze pixels, lighting and patterns to spot AI edits [web:76][web:82].

  • FaceOnLive Deepfake & AI Image Detector: faceonlive.com/deepfake-detector – upload images or videos to check if they are AI‑generated or manipulated [web:76].
  • Deepware Video Scanner: deepware.ai – scan suspicious videos for deepfake signs using specialised detection models [web:73][web:80].
  • AI Image Detector (alternate): aiimagedetector.org – check if a still image is likely AI‑generated [web:81].
Tip: Combine these tools with reverse image search to see where else the same picture appears [web:70][web:71].
